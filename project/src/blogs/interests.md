Hey there! 👋 

So you want to know what gets me up in the morning? What makes my brain buzz with excitement? Buckle up, because we're about to dive into the beautiful chaos that is my life and interests.

## The Professional Side: Building & Breaking Things

Let me start with what pays the bills and feeds my soul professionally. I'm that person who gets genuinely excited about **coding, hacking, and malware research**. Before you raise an eyebrow - yes, I said malware, but hear me out! 

My world revolves around **ethical demonstration of what adversarial actors can do to our systems and data**. I spend my working days figuring out how to outsmart protection mechanisms (EDRs, input validators, rate limits) & misconfigs, not to cause harm, but to make them stronger (prove a point). It's like being a locksmith who tests locks by trying to pick them - except my locks are digital, and the stakes are a bit higher.

> "I find joy in  building and breaking stuff, or breaking what others have made - in an ethical way, of course."

When I'm not diving deep into the **Windows Internals** (yes, I'm one of those people who finds Windows documentation **absolutely boring** but I must do it anyway), I'm usually working on **web applications and API exploitation** on bug bounty platforms. There's nothing quite like the rush of getting a shell access. In every engagement I'm always manifesting a cmd access, it's what fuels my working days. Build something secure, then try to break it. Find the flaw, patch it, then find another one. Rinse and repeat. It never gets old.

## The Human Side: Family and Health

But here's the thing - I'm not just lines of code and exploit chains. I'm a **big family person** at heart. There's something about children's curiosity that reminds me why I fell in love with learning in the first place. That's why I make time to associate with and nurture the upcoming generation. I think this is the reason why I keep an open mind. On days i'm debugging my soul I tend to argue with random people on X as a way of forming connections. People are just intresting.

**Health is wealth** - and I'm not just saying that because it sounds good on a motivational poster. I'm that person who prioritizes the **gym** and a **good diet** above everything else. You can have all the technical skills in the world, but if your body and mind aren't in sync, what's the point? 

My philosophy is simple: take care of your body, and it'll take care of your brain. And my brain needs to be sharp for all those late-night debugging sessions!

## The Explorer: Adventures and Perspectives

**Traveling the world** is my way of debugging my soul. I've been almost everywhere you can imagine - seriously, the only place I haven't been to is Russia and **space** (and if you can hook me up with that, let's definitely [connect!](https://linkedin.com/in/isaacogina)). 

Each new place brings new perspectives, and I'm absolutely obsessed with understanding how different people see the world. Being **open-minded** isn't just a personality trait for me - it's a survival skill. In cybersecurity, the moment you think you know everything is the moment someone finds a vulnerability you never considered.

**Physics** blows my mind on a regular basis. The fact that we can explain the fundamental concepts of our universe through equations and theories. That's the ultimate hack right there. Sometimes I think the universe is just one massive, elegant codebase, and we're all trying to reverse-engineer it.

## The Philosopher: Deep Work and Peace

I'm a firm believer in **deep work** for long-term success in technical subjects, I attribute it to deep work. You can't master assembly language or understand advanced persistent threats by scrolling through social media (no offense). Real expertise comes from those long, uninterrupted sessions where you dive so deep you forget what day it is.

But balance is key. **Nature** is my reset button. Whether it's **taking a walk**, going for a long drive, or just sitting outside and breathing, these moments keep me grounded. Sometimes you need to step away from the screens to see the bigger picture. Life's beautiful outside.

**Geopolitics** fascinates me - the chess game of nations, the complex web of relationships and power dynamics. But here's where I draw a hard line: **I don't and will never endorse war** against other human beings. **Peace is always a better option**. 

We're all part of the same human species, sharing the same planet. If we're going to "go to war" against anything, let it be against ignorance, poverty, and the bugs in our code. Those are battles worth fighting.

## The Bottom Line

At the end of the day, I'm just someone who's endlessly curious about how things work - whether that's a computer system, a human mind, AI (it's really interesting what this search engines on steroids can do) or the laws of physics. I believe in peace as an option, privacy, freedom of access to information , continuous learning and research, taking care of your health, exploring the world, and treating every person you meet as a potential teacher.

Life's too short for boring code, bad health, closed minds, and unnecessary conflicts. Let's build cool stuff, break it responsibly, learn from each other, and maybe grab a coffee/drink while we're at it.

What about you? What drives your curiosity? I'd love to hear your perspective! 🤔
