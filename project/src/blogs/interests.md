# My Interests: The Things That Drive Me

Hey there! 👋 

So you want to know what gets me up in the morning? What makes my brain buzz with excitement? Buckle up, because we're about to dive into the beautiful chaos that is my life and interests.

## The Professional Side: Building & Breaking Things

Let me start with what pays the bills and feeds my soul professionally. I'm that person who gets genuinely excited about **coding, hacking, and malware development**. Before you raise an eyebrow - yes, I said malware development, but hear me out! 

My world revolves around **EDR evasion techniques** and **C2 frameworks**. I spend my days figuring out how to outsmart security systems, not to cause harm, but to make them stronger. It's like being a locksmith who tests locks by trying to pick them - except my locks are digital, and the stakes are a bit higher.

> "I love building and breaking stuff, or breaking what others have made - in an ethical way, of course."

**AI** has become my new playground lately. The intersection of artificial intelligence and cybersecurity? *Chef's kiss* 👨‍🍳💋 There's something magical about teaching machines to think like attackers so we can defend better.

When I'm not diving deep into the **Linux kernel** (yes, I'm one of those people who finds kernel documentation fascinating), I'm usually working on **web applications and API development**. **Offensive testing** keeps things interesting - there's nothing quite like the rush of finding that one vulnerability that everyone else missed.

It's all about that eternal dance between offense and defense. Build something secure, then try to break it. Find the flaw, patch it, then find another one. Rinse and repeat. It never gets old.

## The Human Side: Family, Health, and Adventure

But here's the thing - I'm not just lines of code and exploit chains. I'm a **big family person** at heart. There's something about children's curiosity that reminds me why I fell in love with learning in the first place. That's why I make time to **pass knowledge to institutions**. Watching someone's eyes light up when they finally "get it"? That's better than any successful penetration test.

**Health is wealth** - and I'm not just saying that because it sounds good on a motivational poster. I'm that person who prioritizes the **gym** and health above everything else. You can have all the technical skills in the world, but if your body and mind aren't in sync, what's the point? 

My philosophy is simple: take care of your body, and it'll take care of your brain. And my brain needs to be sharp for all those late-night debugging sessions! 💪

## The Explorer: Adventures and Perspectives

**Traveling the world** is my way of debugging my soul. I've been almost everywhere you can imagine - seriously, the only place I haven't been to is **space** (and if you can hook me up with that, let's definitely connect! 🚀). 

Each new place brings new perspectives, and I'm absolutely obsessed with understanding how different people see the world. Being **open-minded** isn't just a personality trait for me - it's a survival skill. In cybersecurity, the moment you think you know everything is the moment someone finds a vulnerability you never considered.

**Physics** blows my mind on a regular basis. The fact that we can explain the fundamental concepts of our universe through equations and theories? That's the ultimate hack right there. Sometimes I think the universe is just one massive, elegant codebase, and we're all trying to reverse-engineer it.

## The Philosopher: Deep Work and Peace

I'm a firm believer in **deep work** for long-term success in technical subjects. You can't master assembly language or understand advanced persistent threats by scrolling through social media. Real expertise comes from those long, uninterrupted sessions where you dive so deep you forget what day it is.

But balance is key. **Nature** is my reset button. Whether it's **taking a walk**, going for a drive, or just sitting outside and breathing, these moments keep me grounded. Sometimes you need to step away from the screens to see the bigger picture.

**Geopolitics** fascinates me - the chess game of nations, the complex web of relationships and power dynamics. But here's where I draw a hard line: **I don't and will never endorse war** against other human beings. **Peace is always the way out**. 

We're all part of the same human species, sharing the same planet. If we're going to "go to war" against anything, let it be against ignorance, poverty, and the bugs in our code. Those are battles worth fighting.

## The Bottom Line

At the end of the day, I'm just someone who's endlessly curious about how things work - whether that's a computer system, a human mind, or the laws of physics. I believe in ethical hacking, continuous learning, taking care of your health, exploring the world, and treating every person you meet as a potential teacher.

Life's too short for boring code, bad health, closed minds, and unnecessary conflicts. Let's build cool stuff, break it responsibly, learn from each other, and maybe grab a coffee while we're at it.

What about you? What drives your curiosity? I'd love to hear your perspective! 🤔
